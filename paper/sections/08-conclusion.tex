\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
A parameter-free normalisation restores valid, $\alpha$-responsive coverage
on overconfident navigation policies where the standard scores collapse, uses
the whole calibration set, and spends none of the compute. The guarantee
behind it is proved in full. At every level the operator selects, the correct
action survives inside the set for the whole walk, in finite samples, with
exchangeable episodes as the single premise. Deployment stresses exactly that premise, and we measure its price under
the seen-to-unseen move instead of assuming it away. The lesson for robot
builders is to reach for the cheap signal, spend operator attention where the
sets flag it, and recalibrate inside each building to buy the guarantee
back.
