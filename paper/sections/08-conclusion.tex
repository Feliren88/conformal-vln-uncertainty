\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
A parameter-free normalisation restores valid, $\alpha$-responsive coverage
on overconfident navigation policies where the standard scores collapse, at
zero training cost. The guarantee is proved in full: at every level the
operator selects, the correct action survives inside the set for the whole
walk, in finite samples, under exchangeable episodes alone, the one premise
deployment stresses and whose price we measure rather than assume
away. The lesson for robot builders: reach for the cheap signal, spend
operator attention where the sets flag it, and recalibrate inside each
building to buy the guarantee back.
